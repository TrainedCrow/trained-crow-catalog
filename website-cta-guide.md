# Website CTA Guide

A good CTA does more than ask for a click. It makes the next step feel clear, useful, and low-friction.

## CTA Clarity

Use wording that tells the buyer what happens next.

Weak: `Submit`

Clearer: `Get the checklist`, `View the kit`, `Start the review`

## CTA Placement

Place the main CTA where it follows enough context. If a CTA appears before the buyer understands the offer, it can feel premature.

## CTA Confidence

Near important CTAs, add reassurance such as:

- what is included
- what happens after payment
- product format
- support contact
- safe-use disclaimer

## CTA Friction

Reduce friction by removing competing actions, unclear button text, and surprise steps.
